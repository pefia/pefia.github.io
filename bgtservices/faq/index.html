Tree Services

If your tree is subject to a Tree Preservation Order (TPO) or is within a Conservation Area, you will need to apply to your local council for permission before any work can start. Trees in Conservation Areas are protected even without a TPO. We can advise you on this when we carry out the free quote visit.

Most tree work can be carried out year-round. The dormant season (October to March) is often preferred for structural pruning and crown work as it's easier to see the tree's framework without leaves. Urgent work — such as removing dangerous trees or storm-damaged branches — should be done as soon as possible, regardless of season.

All timber and green waste is removed from your property as standard. We can leave timber in manageable rounds for firewood if you prefer — just let us know. Chippings from smaller branches can be left on site as a mulch, or removed along with everything else.

Yes, when carried out by qualified professionals. We always assess each situation carefully, plan the safest method of removal (which may involve sectional dismantling rather than felling), and take appropriate precautions to protect your property.

Yes, we have experience working near overhead cables. For trees that are directly intertwined with power lines, we may need to liaise with your network operator — we can advise on this during the quote visit.

Garden Services

This depends on your garden's size and planting. Most residential gardens benefit from fortnightly visits during the growing season (April to October) and monthly in the winter months. We offer flexible schedules tailored to your needs.

A standard maintenance visit typically includes lawn mowing and edging, border weeding and tidying, pruning of shrubs and plants as needed, and general rubbish clearance. We tailor the scope of each visit to what your garden needs at that time of year.

Yes. Garden clearance — including heavily overgrown and neglected gardens — is one of our most common services. We'll clear everything, remove all waste, and leave the ground ready for replanting, turfing, or landscaping.

We trim all common UK hedge species including leylandii, laurel, beech, hornbeam, privet, yew, holly, box, photinia, and mixed native hedgerows. Different species need different timing and techniques, which we always account for.

Both. We're happy to quote for one-off jobs — clearances, one-time hedge trims, single-visit maintenance — as well as setting up regular maintenance contracts.
